Description

Have you ever wondered how deliciously crunchy a cockroach might be but was never ever able to fullfill that dream due to unrelenting societal standards?

Well fear not today for this recipe will help you concoct the most satisfying and crispy sugar-coated candy with the same level, if not more, of crunchiness as a wild cockroach hiding under your home's refrigerator.

It's very simple to make that even cockroaches could probably be able to if only they were blessed with limbs like us superior human mammals.

Well what are we waiting for? Let's jump right in!

Ingredients

  • Any fruit of your liking
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Skewers

Steps

    1. Prepare the Fruit

    1. Wash the fruit and completely dry them, otherwise the sugar won't stick.
    2. Remove any undesirable parts of the fruit diminishing the texture and taste of the fruit, ie. leaves, stems, seeds.
    3. If necessary, cut your fruit into bite-size pieces.
    4. Poke your fruit pieces onto the skewers. For best results, limit to 3 pieces per skewer.

    2. Make the Sugar Coating

    1. Add sugar and water in a ratio of 2:1 in a pot.
    2. Heat up the pot to low to medium heat.
    3. ***DO NOT STIR THE POT because the air bubbles causes the sugar to crystalize
    4. Keep heating up that shit until it thicc like some nice old syrup. The color should be giving amber.

    3. Test the Sugar Coating

    1. Before you start pouring that shit onto your delightful fruit cutlery, you gotta test your sugar sugar.
    2. Do this by drizzling a spoonful of syrup into an ice water bath.
    3. If the sugar hardens within seconds and shatter when you attempt to exert pressure via what I hope are sanitized fingertips, that when you know it's ready.
    4. If your sugar syrup fails to pass the test, you gotta let it simmer in the pot a little more dear. Patience is sweet and savory.

    4. Now Some Fun shit

    1. Simple: Dip your fruit skewers inside your sugar sugar and SWIRL that mf around.
    2. Helpful tip: when you take your fruit skewers out from the pot, twirl it so that the excess sugar don't drip over the side and onto your delicate skin.
    3. Immediately dunk your sugar coated skewer inside an ice water bath to harden the burning sticky sugar coating to a satisfyingly crunchy one

  5. Enjoy!
